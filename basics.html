A great kitchen companion for your cooking adventures. Master the fundamentals before you reach for the stove.

01

Equipment

A kitchen should have an assortment of equipment for at least basic functionality. Different people will prefer some equipment over others, and what some may perceive as indispensable may only be of convenience to others.

01.1

Knives

Knives are essential in food preparation. Choosing which knives to buy can be difficult from the abundant choices — from different shapes, sizes, blade materials and handle materials. Here are the essentials.

8–12"

Chef Knife

General purpose. The workhorse of the kitchen. Chop, slice, mince, and dice.

5–8"

Santoku

Similar to the Chef Knife. Excels at precision slicing, dicing, and mincing.

2.5–4"

Paring Knife

Small tasks. To peel, to core, and for detailed garnish work.

8–10"

Serrated Knife

Items with hard exterior and soft interior, such as bread and tomatoes.

6"

Cleaver

Tough edge. Split bones, dense cartilage, and thick cuts of meat.

5–6"

Boning Knife

Thin flexible blade. Remove bones from meat with precision.

02

Space Organisation

Avoid cluttering the countertop, drawers, or cupboard with ingredients, utensils or anything else.

Use racks, shelves, or other organising units where necessary to keep order in the kitchen space. Having a clear countertop will allow more space for food preparation and organised drawers and cupboards will make items easier to locate.

A countertop with easy access to the sink and the stovetop is recommended. Food preparation generally requires some cleaning up, thus a sink nearby does help. The same concept applies to ingredients, cookware, and utensils. Keep a select handful of items that are used regularly near the preparation area.

03

Techniques

Getting comfortable using a knife properly will make it quicker, safer, and more enjoyable to cook regularly. The two most important things to learn are to always use a sharp knife and make sure to stabilise the cutting board.

Pinch Grip

Place the middle finger behind the knife bolster or guard, with the ring and little finger around the handle, while pinching the blade with the thumb and forefinger. A safe hold even with slightly damp hands.

Claw Grip

The other hand holds the ingredient being cut. Hold on to the ingredient with thumb and fingers, and keep fingertips curled inward to avoid getting hit by the knife's blade.

04

Food & Kitchen Safety

Properly handling and preparing food is crucial to keep everybody safe. Before handling any food or equipment, hands should be washed thoroughly with soap and water.

Temperature Control

Meat should not be kept at room temperature for more than 2 hours.

Cross-Contamination

Thoroughly wash vegetables, especially root varieties. Use separate cutting boards for meat and vegetables.

Thawing

Thaw meat from the freezer properly before cooking. Recommended: in the refrigerator or submerged in cold water.

Cleaning

Clean sponges after each use. The sink is subjected to grease, dirt, and food residue — sanitise this area regularly.
