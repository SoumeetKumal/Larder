# Meal Planning Philosophy
*The Bridge Between Knowledge and Execution*

## 1. Core Purpose
Within the Compass stewardship framework, the Meal Planner is the execution engine of the Food & Health Ecosystem. 
If the **Larder CMS** is "What we know" and **Fit Track** is "What we achieved", the **Meal Planner** answers: *"What are we doing today?"*

It exists to bridge the Shared Knowledge Layer (Recipes, Ingredients) with the realities of household management, schedule constraints, and personal capacity.

## 2. Guiding Principles
- **Adaptability over Rigidity:** Life involves long shifts, fatigue, and spontaneity. A rigid plan that breaks when life happens is a failed plan. The planner must bend.
- **Frictionless Execution:** The system exists to reduce cognitive load at 6:00 PM. It makes the faithful stewardship of food the easiest choice.
- **Realistic Household Alignment:** It must support a hybrid household reality—accommodating both structured meal prep and spontaneous, from-scratch cooking.

## 3. Core Mechanisms

### A. Scheduling & Flexibility
- **The "Eating Out" Placeholder:** Eating outside the home is treated as a first-class citizen in the planner, not a failure of the plan. Inserting an "Eating Out" or "Takeout" placeholder maintains the integrity of the schedule and keeps caloric/budget expectations grounded in reality.
- **Spontaneous vs. Planned:** The planner does not demand 100% schedule density. Days can be left blank for spontaneous cooking, relying on pantry staples rather than strict adherence to a pre-selected recipe.

### B. Smart Shopping Lists
- **Variable Time Horizons:** Shopping list generation is entirely modular. You can generate a list based on:
  - A single meal (quick grocery run)
  - A single day
  - A week of planned meals
  - A month of bulk prepping
- **Ingredient Aggregation:** The system will automatically aggregate shared ingredients across planned recipes (e.g., combining 2 onions for Tuesday's curry and 1 onion for Wednesday's pasta into a single list item).

### C. Flexible Pantry Integration
- **Opt-In Tracking:** Attempting to track every grain of rice or pinch of salt leads to system abandonment. Pantry integration is strictly flexible.
- **Tracked vs. Untracked Toggle:** Ingredients will have a simple designation. You can choose to actively track high-value or highly-perishable items (like specific cuts of meat or fresh vegetables) while leaving staples (spices, flour, oil) as "Untracked" so they don't clutter the system.

### D. Prep Modularity
- **Component Prep:** The planner acknowledges that meal prep isn't always making 5 identical Tupperware meals. It supports planning for "components" (e.g., cooking a large batch of rice or marinating chicken on Sunday) to be used flexibly in spontaneous meals throughout the week.

## 4. Ecosystem Integration
- **Reads from Shared Knowledge (Larder CMS):** Pulls recipes, ingredient data, and nutrition facts to inform the plan.
- **Pushes to Tracking (Fit Track):** When a planned meal is executed, it can theoretically be pushed to Fit Track with a single click as a completed log, eliminating double data-entry.
